## 1. The Central Question

### 1.1 Mathematical Context

The rational numbers admit exactly two types of completion — ways of "filling the gaps" between fractions to create a continuous number system. The familiar real numbers represent one type (Archimedean). For each prime number, there exists another type called a non-Archimedean completion. These are not alternative interpretations but mathematically distinct number systems with different geometries and different rules for measuring distance. This exhaustive classification was proven by Alexander Ostrowski in 1916.

The product of a rational number's "size" measured in the real numbers and its "size" measured in every non-Archimedean completion equals exactly one. This identity — the adelic product formula $|q|_\infty \prod_p |q|_p = 1$ — is the only universal constraint linking these otherwise independent number systems.

### 1.2 Physical Motivation

Modern physics is built on the real numbers. Spacetime coordinates, field values, and coupling constants are all taken to be real-valued. But every measurement ever performed has yielded a rational number with finite precision. The choice to complete the rationals using the real numbers rather than non-Archimedean alternatives is an assumption, not an empirical necessity.

If physical laws must be consistently expressible across all completions of the rational numbers — if nature does not arbitrarily privilege the real numbers — then this consistency requirement might constrain the form and perhaps the numerical values of physical couplings. The project's central question was:

> Does the adelic consistency condition constrain dimensionless physical constants?

### 1.3 The Historical Context

In 1987, Peter Freund and Edward Witten proposed that the Veneziano amplitude — the first string scattering amplitude, discovered in 1968 — could be factorized into an adelic product: $A_\infty(s,t) \cdot \prod_p A_p(s,t) = 1$. The entire scattering amplitude, summed over all completions of the rational numbers, equals exactly one. Three and a half decades passed without this proposal being developed into a testable physical framework. This project picked up that thread.

A structural constraint says "the coupling must remain finite at all scales" — a condition satisfied by a continuous family of possible values. A numerical prediction says "the coupling at the Z-boson mass is 1/127.9" — a single number. The adelic framework operates at the former level, not the latter.
