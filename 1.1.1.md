 **"Adelic string amplitudes" by Peter G. O. Freund and Edward Witten** (*Physics Letters B*, Volume 199, Issue 2, 1987, pp. 191-194). 

To satisfy the "key unknown," here are the exact mathematical normalizations and the explicit formulation of the $p$-adic Veneziano amplitude directly from the original 1987 Freund-Witten literature:

### 1. The Normalization Factors
For the exact Freund-Witten adelic product formula to equal exactly $1$, several specific normalizations are imposed on the $p$-adic side:

*   **Measure Normalization:** The $p$-adic amplitude is defined as an integral over the field of $p$-adic numbers $\mathbb{Q}_p$. The additive Haar measure $dx$ is uniquely normalized such that the compact ring of $p$-adic integers, $\mathbb{Z}_p$, has a volume of exactly unity:
    $$ \int_{\mathbb{Z}_p} dx = 1 $$
*   **Kinematic Normalization (Regge Trajectories):** The parameters $a, b, c$ corresponding to the Mandelstam variables (via the Regge trajectories) must be normalized symmetrically so that they sum to $1$. Usually written as $\alpha(s), \alpha(t), \alpha(u)$, Freund and Witten enforce the strict kinematic constraint:
    $$ \alpha(s) + \alpha(t) + \alpha(u) = 1 $$
*   **Adelic Coupling Constant:** No stray dimensionful coefficients are added to the individual $p$-adic amplitudes. Instead, the "adelic coupling constant" formed by the infinite product of real and all $p$-adic local string coupling constants is normalized to $1$.

### 2. The Explicit $p$-adic Veneziano Amplitude
With the measure normalized as above, Freund and Witten define the 4-point open string $p$-adic Veneziano amplitude structurally identically to the real string, but integrating over the $p$-adic line instead of $\mathbb{R}$:
$$ A_p(s, t) = \int_{\mathbb{Q}_p} |x|_p^{\alpha(s)-1} |1-x|_p^{\alpha(t)-1} dx $$

Using the Gel'fand-Graev local $p$-adic Gamma function—which arises directly from evaluating integrals of $p$-adic multiplicative characters over $\mathbb{Q}_p$—this amplitude can be solved analytically. The $p$-adic Gamma function is normalized as:
$$ \Gamma_p(x) = \frac{1 - p^{x-1}}{1 - p^{-x}} $$

Because of the identity $\Gamma_p(x)\Gamma_p(1-x) = 1$ and the kinematic normalization $\alpha(s)+\alpha(t) = 1 - \alpha(u)$, the evaluated integral becomes beautifully symmetric over all three scattering channels (s, t, and u):
$$ A_p(s, t) = \Gamma_p(\alpha(s)) \Gamma_p(\alpha(t)) \Gamma_p(\alpha(u)) $$

### 3. The Freund-Witten Product Formula
Freund and Witten demonstrated that if you take the product of the standard real string amplitude $A_\infty(s,t)$ and the string amplitudes evaluated over all prime fields $p$, all local scattering parameters miraculously cancel out:
$$ A_\infty(s, t) \prod_{p \text{ prime}} A_p(s, t) = 1 $$

**Why does this require no other arbitrary constants?** 
It fundamentally links string theory to number theory. The local $p$-adic factor $\Gamma_p(x)$ can be rewritten in terms of local Euler factors of the Riemann zeta function: $\zeta_p(x) = \frac{1}{1-p^{-x}}$. The infinite product over all primes builds the global Riemann zeta function $\zeta(x)$. 

When the real/Archimedean amplitude $A_\infty(s,t)$ is multiplied against the infinite product of $p$-adic amplitudes, the expression perfectly collapses into the classic **functional equation of the Riemann zeta function**, leaving exactly $1$.
