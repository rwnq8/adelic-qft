## 1. Executive Summary

Module D2 tested the hypothesis — suggested in M6 and elevated to a "discovery" in 2.6.md — that ratios of particle masses correspond to ratios of consecutive Riemann zeta zeros. This was the most speculative claim in the project and the one most in need of rigorous statistical testing.

**Result: The zeta zero mass ratio correlations are statistical coincidences, not physical predictions.** None survive Bonferroni correction at α = 0.05 (threshold = 2.86 × 10⁻⁷). A null model with random mass spectra produces comparable match rates. With 175,000 total comparisons, coincidental matches within 2% tolerance are expected by chance.

## 2. Methodology

### 2.1 Data

- **Zeta zeros:** 500 zeros computed via mpmath (range: 14.13 to 811.18)
- **Mean normalized spacing:** 1.0016 (expected 1.0)
- **Particle masses:** PDG 2024 central values (7 ratios tested)

### 2.2 Search Protocol

For each mass ratio \(R_m = m_1/m_2\):

1. Compute all zeta zero ratios \(R_z(i, d) = \gamma_{i+d} / \gamma_i\) for offsets \(d \in [1, 50]\)
2. Find the pair \((i, d)\) that minimizes \(|R_z - R_m| / R_m\)
3. Record the match if within 2% tolerance

### 2.3 Statistical Corrections

- **Bonferroni correction:** α_adj = 0.05 / N_comparisons = 2.86 × 10⁻⁷ (175,000 total comparisons)
- **Null model:** 1000 trials with 7 random log-uniform masses → 21 random ratios each

### 3.2 Statistical Significance

| Test | Result |
|:-----|:-------|
| Total comparisons | 175,000 |
| Bonferroni threshold | 2.86 × 10⁻⁷ |
| Matches within 2% | 3/7 |
| Matches surviving Bonferroni | **0** |
| Null model avg matches | 3.35 per trial |
| Observed vs. null | NOT significantly different |

### 3.3 Why the "Exact" Matches Are Coincidences

The apparent precision (0.001% error for m_Z/m_W and m_top/m_H) is an artifact of:
1. **Large search space:** 500 zeros × 50 offsets = 25,000 ratios per target, 175,000 total
2. **Continuous matching:** Any real number can be approximated to arbitrary precision by a ratio of real numbers given sufficient search space
3. **No physical mechanism:** There is no theoretical reason why particle masses — determined by Yukawa couplings to the Higgs and QCD confinement — should correspond to ratios of zeros of the Riemann zeta function

The null model confirms: random masses drawn from a log-uniform distribution over the zeta range produce an average of 3.35 matches per trial (vs. our 3 observed), well within one standard deviation.

## 5. Scaling Considerations

### 5.1 Computation Time

| Zeros | Time | Rate (avg) | Feasible? |
|:-----:|:----:|:----------:|:----------|
| 100 | 7.3s | 14 z/s | ✅ |
| 500 | 104s | 4.8 z/s | ✅ |
| 2000 | ~15 min | ~2 z/s | ⚠️ timeout |
| 10⁵ | ~2 hr | ~14 z/s | ❌ requires batch |

The rate drops from 14 z/s (n=100) to 4.8 z/s (n=500) to ~2 z/s (n=2000) due to the O(n) cost per zero. For 10⁵ zeros, a batch/checkpointed approach over several hours would be required.

### 5.2 Statistical Power

Even with 10⁵ zeros, the statistical challenge remains: the Bonferroni threshold becomes even stricter (10⁵ zeros × 50 offsets × 7 ratios ≈ 3.5 × 10⁷ comparisons, α_adj ≈ 1.4 × 10⁻⁹). Any "match" would need to be extraordinarily precise to survive correction.

**Recommendation:** The zeta zero mass ratio hypothesis is probably untestable with computational methods alone — it would need a theoretical derivation of WHY specific zero ratios should correspond to specific mass ratios before it could be tested. As a pure pattern-matching exercise, it's statistically indistinguishable from chance.
