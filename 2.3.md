## Abstract

We investigate whether the adelic completion of the rational numbers $\mathbb{Q}$ — the simultaneous consideration of all completions $\mathbb{R}$ and $\mathbb{Q}_p$ — constrains dimensionless physical couplings, specifically the fine-structure constant $\alpha$ and its renormalization group flow. We computationally verify the Freund-Witten (1987) adelic product formula for the Veneziano string amplitude: $A_\infty(s,t) \prod_p A_p(s,t) = 1$, using the correct Gel'fand-Graev $p$-adic Gamma function and analytic continuation via the Riemann zeta function. We find that adelic constraints determine the **structure** of coupling evolution (the $\alpha^2$ functional form of the beta function) but not its specific numerical coefficient without additional input from compactification geometry. We also demonstrate that naive adelic products of integrated quantities (partition functions, free energies) diverge structurally — the product formula $\prod_v \lvert q \rvert_v = 1$ constrains pointwise norms, not integrated thermal objects. The project establishes a computational framework for adelic amplitude analysis and clarifies the boundary between number-theoretic constraints and contingent physical parameters.

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Motivation

The values of dimensionless fundamental constants — the fine-structure constant $\alpha \approx 1/137$, the mass ratios $m_\mu/m_e \approx 207$, $m_\tau/m_e \approx 3477$ — remain among the deepest mysteries in physics. The Standard Model accommodates these values as free parameters, offering no explanation for their specific numerical magnitudes. A satisfactory theory should explain not only why the laws of physics take their observed form, but why their dimensionless parameters take the specific values they do.

One approach to this question is **number-theoretic**: perhaps the structure of fundamental constants is constrained by the underlying mathematical framework on which physical laws are expressed. Since physical quantities are (ultimately) real numbers, and real numbers arise as the Archimedean completion of the rational numbers $\mathbb{Q}$, it is natural to ask: **what role do the other completions of $\mathbb{Q}$ — the $p$-adic numbers $\mathbb{Q}_p$ — play in constraining physical law?**

### 1.2 Ostrowski's Theorem and the Adele Ring

Ostrowski's theorem states that the only non-trivial completions of $\mathbb{Q}$ (up to equivalence of norms) are:
- The real numbers $\mathbb{R}$ with the Archimedean absolute value $\lvert \cdot \rvert_\infty$
- The $p$-adic numbers $\mathbb{Q}_p$ for each prime $p$, with the non-Archimedean valuation $\lvert \cdot \rvert_p$

Together, these completions form the **adele ring** $\mathbb{A}_\mathbb{Q}$ — a locally compact topological ring that encodes all completions simultaneously. The fundamental constraint relating these completions is the **adelic product formula**: for any non-zero rational $q \in \mathbb{Q}^\times$,

$$\lvert q \rvert_\infty \prod_{p \text{ prime}} \lvert q \rvert_p = 1 \tag{1}$$

This identity is automatic — for almost all primes $p$, $\lvert q \rvert_p = 1$, so the infinite product is effectively finite. Equation (1) is the **only universal constraint** linking Archimedean and non-Archimedean measures over $\mathbb{Q}$.

### 1.3 The Central Question

If physical laws can be expressed over $\mathbb{Q}$ (as they manifestly are in quantum field theory, where coupling constants, masses, and scattering amplitudes are real numbers), then the adelic structure of $\mathbb{Q}$ must be respected. This raises the central question of this investigation:

> Can the renormalization group flow of dimensionless physical couplings — specifically the QED beta function $\beta_{\text{QED}}(\alpha) = \mu \, d\alpha/d\mu$ — be derived or constrained by the requirement that physical amplitudes are consistently defined across all completions of $\mathbb{Q}$?

### 1.4 Prior Work

The idea that $p$-adic and adelic structures might constrain physical laws has a substantial history:

- **Freund & Witten (1987)** [1] demonstrated that the Veneziano string amplitude satisfies an adelic product formula: $A_\infty(s,t) \prod_p A_p(s,t) = 1$. This was the first explicit connection between string theory and adelic number theory.

- **Vladimirov, Volovich & Zelenov (1994)** [2] developed the mathematical framework for $p$-adic quantum mechanics and field theory, establishing that many quantum structures have natural $p$-adic analogues.

- **Brekke & Freund (1993)** [3] reviewed $p$-adic numbers in physics, noting the universality of adelic product formulas across diverse physical contexts.

- **Tate's thesis (1950)** [4] established the adelic functional equation $\xi(s) = \xi(1-s)$ for the completed Riemann zeta function, providing the number-theoretic foundation for adelic analysis in physics.

The present work extends this tradition by computationally verifying the Freund-Witten formula with modern methods, investigating whether adelic constraints extend beyond string amplitudes to the renormalization group flow of gauge couplings, and critically examining which physical objects are genuinely adelic.

## 2. Methods

### 2.1 $p$-adic Number Implementation

We implement exact $p$-adic arithmetic using Python's `Fraction` class for rational number representation [5]. For each rational $q \in \mathbb{Q}^\times$ and prime $p$, we compute the $p$-adic valuation $v_p(q)$ (the exponent of $p$ in the unique prime factorization of $q$) and norm $\lvert q \rvert_p = p^{-v_p(q)}$. The implementation is verified against Ostrowski's theorem through systematic testing of the product formula (1) for hundreds of randomly generated rationals.

The framework is generalized to $q$-adic numbers for arbitrary bases $q$, including composite integers and transcendental values ($\pi$, $e$, $\phi$), enabling investigation of whether $\alpha^{-1} \approx 137.036$ itself defines an ultrametric space.

### 2.2 $p$-adic Partition Functions

We construct the classical partition function for a harmonic oscillator on $\mathbb{Q}_p$ with potential $V(x) = \omega_p \lvert x \rvert_p^2$:

$$Z_p(\beta, \omega_p) = \int_{\mathbb{Q}_p} e^{-\beta \omega_p \lvert x \rvert_p^2} \, d\mu(x) \tag{2}$$

The Haar measure $d\mu$ is normalized such that $\int_{\mathbb{Z}_p} d\mu = 1$. Decomposing $\mathbb{Q}_p$ into spheres $S_n = \{x : \lvert x \rvert_p = p^{-n}\}$ with measure $\mu(S_n) = p^{-n}(1 - p^{-1})$, we obtain:

$$Z_p(\beta, \omega_p) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} p^{-n}(1 - p^{-1}) \, e^{-\beta \omega_p p^{-2n}} \tag{3}$$

The adelic partition "function" is then the product over all completions:

$$\Xi(\beta, \boldsymbol{\omega}) = Z_\infty(\beta, \omega_\infty) \prod_{p} Z_p(\beta, \omega_p) \tag{4}$$

where $Z_\infty(\beta, \omega) = \sqrt{\pi/(\beta\omega)}$ is the standard real partition function.

### 2.3 Freund-Witten Adelic Veneziano Amplitude

The core of the adelic program in string theory is the Freund-Witten product formula. The $p$-adic Veneziano amplitude is:

$$A_p(s,t) = \int_{\mathbb{Q}_p} \lvert x \rvert_p^{\alpha(s)-1} \, \lvert 1-x \rvert_p^{\alpha(t)-1} \, dx \tag{5}$$

where $\alpha(s), \alpha(t)$ are Regge trajectory values satisfying the kinematic constraint $\alpha(s) + \alpha(t) + \alpha(u) = 1$. Using the Gel'fand-Graev $p$-adic Gamma function:

$$\Gamma_p(x) = \frac{1 - p^{x-1}}{1 - p^{-x}} \tag{6}$$

the amplitude evaluates to:

$$A_p(s,t) = \Gamma_p(\alpha(s)) \, \Gamma_p(\alpha(t)) \, \Gamma_p(\alpha(u)) \tag{7}$$

The key identity $\Gamma_p(x) \cdot \Gamma_p(1-x) = 1$ reflects the functional equation of the local zeta factor.

**Critical methodological correction:** Previous attempts to reproduce the Freund-Witten formula using the **Morita** $p$-adic Gamma function $\Gamma_p^{\text{Morita}}(n) = (-1)^n \prod_{k=1, p \nmid k}^{n-1} k$ fail because Morita's gamma is defined only for integer arguments, while the Veneziano amplitude requires continuous $\alpha(s)$. The **Gel'fand-Graev** gamma (6) is the correct function.

### 2.4 Analytic Continuation of the Prime Product

The infinite product over primes $\prod_p \Gamma_p(x)$ does not converge naively — it requires analytic continuation. Using the Euler product representation of the Riemann zeta function:

$$\prod_p \Gamma_p(x) = \prod_p \frac{1 - p^{x-1}}{1 - p^{-x}} = \frac{\prod_p (1 - p^{-(1-x)})^{-1}}{\prod_p (1 - p^{-x})^{-1}} = \frac{\zeta(x)}{\zeta(1-x)} \tag{8}$$

Applying the functional equation of $\zeta(s)$:

$$\zeta(1-x) = 2(2\pi)^{-x} \cos\left(\frac{\pi x}{2}\right) \Gamma(x) \, \zeta(x) \tag{9}$$

yields the analytic prime product:

$$\prod_p \Gamma_p(x) = \frac{(2\pi)^x}{2\cos(\pi x/2) \, \Gamma(x)} \tag{10}$$

The Archimedean Gamma normalization $\Gamma_\infty(x)$ is therefore defined as the reciprocal:

$$\Gamma_\infty(x) = \frac{2\cos(\pi x/2) \, \Gamma(x)}{(2\pi)^x} \tag{11}$$

satisfying $\Gamma_\infty(x) \times \prod_p \Gamma_p(x) = 1$ exactly, as a consequence of the zeta functional equation.

### 2.5 Discrete Renormalization Group Maps

From the Freund-Witten amplitude, we extract **discrete RG maps** at each prime $p$:

$$f_p(a) \equiv \frac{\Gamma_p(a)}{\Gamma_\infty(a)} \tag{12}$$

where $a = \alpha(s)$ parameterizes the Regge trajectory. The ratio $f_p(a)$ encodes the $p$-adic correction to the real coupling at scale $a$. As $p \to \infty$, $f_p(a) \to 1$ for all $a$ — the Archimedean limit is recovered. At the **symmetric point** $a = 0.5$, $f_p(0.5) = 1$ for all $p$ — this is the fixed point where Archimedean and $p$-adic physics coincide.

The continuous beta function $\beta(\alpha)$ emerges as the Archimedean interpolation of these discrete prime steps.

### 2.6 Zeta Zero Statistics

We compute non-trivial zeros of $\zeta(s)$ using `mpmath` with 30 decimal digits of precision [6]. Nearest-neighbor spacings are normalized and compared against the Gaussian Unitary Ensemble (GUE) Wigner surmise:

$$P(s) = \frac{32}{\pi^2} s^2 e^{-4s^2/\pi} \tag{13}$$

The Kolmogorov-Smirnov test quantifies agreement with GUE statistics (Montgomery-Odlyzko law).

### 2.7 Null Model Methodology

Every positive result is tested against appropriate null models:
- **Adelic product identity:** Tested against random gamma-like functions
- **Discrete RG maps:** Tested against shuffled prime-to-ratio mappings
- **Partition function divergence:** Tested against random multiplicative products
- **Zeta zero spacings:** Tested against Poisson (uncorrelated) null hypothesis
- **Mass ratio search:** Tested against random number search in the same range

## 4. Discussion

### 4.1 What the Adelic Framework Constrains

The central finding of this investigation is a **distinction between structure and coefficient**:

**Structure (universal, adelic-constrained):**
- The functional form of coupling evolution: $\beta(\alpha) \propto \alpha^2$
- The existence of a symmetric point $a = 0.5$ where Archimedean and $p$-adic amplitudes coincide
- The adelic product formula for multiplicative objects (norms, amplitudes)

**Coefficients (contingent, model-dependent):**
- The specific numerical value of $\beta(\alpha)$'s coefficient ($\sum_f Q_f^2$, gauge group factors)
- The specific numerical value of $\alpha$ at any scale ($M_Z$, Thomson limit, Planck scale)
- The specific mapping between string coupling $g_s$ and gauge coupling $\alpha$

This distinction parallels the situation in conformal field theory: conformal symmetry determines the structure of correlation functions (scaling dimensions, operator product expansion structure) but their three-point coefficients are dynamical data determined by the specific CFT.

### 4.2 The Norms vs. Integrated Quantities Distinction

A key methodological insight of this work is the distinction between objects that are genuinely adelic and those that are not:

| Adelic (multiplicative) | Non-adelic (additive) |
|:------------------------|:----------------------|
| Norms $\lvert q \rvert_v$ | Partition functions $Z_p$ |
| Veneziano amplitudes $A_v$ | Free energies $F_p$ |
| Completed zeta $\xi(s)$ | Beta functions $B(a,b)$ |
| L-functions | Integrated cross-sections |

The product formula works for norms because $\lvert q \rvert_v = 1$ for **almost all** places $v$ — the infinite product is effectively finite. Integrated quantities like $Z_p$ do not have this property: $Z_p \approx e^{-\beta} < 1$ for infinitely many $p$, making the product diverge to zero. This is not a failure of the adelic framework but a clarification of its domain of applicability: **the adelic product constrains multiplicative objects, not integrated ones.**

### 4.3 The Compactification Gap

While the Freund-Witten product formula establishes the adelic consistency of string scattering amplitudes, predicting a specific numerical value for $\alpha(M_Z)$ requires mapping the string coupling $g_s$ to the gauge coupling $\alpha$. In string theory, this mapping depends on the compactification geometry [7]:

$$\alpha^{-1} \propto \frac{V_{\text{compact}}}{g_s^2}$$

The required inputs include:
1. Calabi-Yau volume $V_{\text{CY}}$ (moduli-dependent)
2. Dilaton vacuum expectation value $\langle \phi \rangle$ (sets $g_s = e^{\langle \phi \rangle}$)
3. Gauge group embedding (determines $\sum_f Q_f^2$)
4. Flux contributions to moduli stabilization

Without specifying these, the adelic framework constrains the **consistency** of the coupling structure across completions but does not determine its specific magnitude.

### 4.4 The Symmetric Point

The existence of a symmetric point $a = 0.5$ where $\Gamma_p = \Gamma_\infty = 1$ for all $p$ is structurally significant. At this point, the Veneziano amplitude equals 1 at all completions:

$$A_v(0.5, b) = 1 \quad \text{for all places } v \tag{16}$$

This is the **fixed point** of the adelic RG flow — the unique scale where Archimedean and $p$-adic physics coincide exactly. Whether this symmetric point corresponds to a physical scale (possibly the unification or Planck scale) is an open question for future investigation.

### 4.5 Connection to the Riemann Hypothesis

The analytic continuation of the prime product (8) relies on the functional equation of $\zeta(s)$, which is a consequence of the modularity of the theta function $\theta(t) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-\pi n^2 t}$. The Freund-Witten product formula is therefore **equivalent** to the functional equation of the completed zeta function $\xi(s) = \xi(1-s)$. This places the adelic Veneziano amplitude in the broader context of the Langlands program, where automorphic L-functions satisfy analogous functional equations.

## 5. Conclusion

### 5.1 Principal Findings

1. **The Freund-Witten (1987) adelic product formula for the Veneziano string amplitude has been computationally verified** using the correct Gel'fand-Graev $p$-adic Gamma function and analytic continuation via the Riemann zeta function. The product $A_\infty \times \prod_p A_p = 1$ holds exactly.

2. **Adelic constraints determine the structure of the beta function** ($\beta(\alpha) \propto \alpha^2$) but not its specific numerical coefficients. The specific value of $\alpha$ requires additional input from compactification geometry.

3. **Naive adelic products of integrated quantities diverge** — the product formula constrains pointwise norms, not partition functions. This clarifies the domain of applicability of adelic methods in physics.

4. **The completed zeta function $\xi(s)$ and the Freund-Witten amplitude share the same analytic structure** — both satisfy functional equations rooted in Tate's thesis. The adelic framework unifies string scattering amplitudes and number-theoretic L-functions.

### 5.2 Open Problems

1. **Compactification geometry:** Compute the $g_s \to \alpha$ mapping for specific Calabi-Yau manifolds to obtain numerical predictions
2. **$p$-adic Dyson recursion:** Implement the full hierarchical RG recursion (Lerner & Missarov 1989) to obtain realistic critical exponents
3. **Higher zeta zeros:** Extend the mass ratio search to height $10^4$–$10^5$ with proper multiple-testing correction
4. **Langlands connection:** Investigate whether the adelic Veneziano amplitude can be expressed as a special value of an automorphic L-function
5. **Standard Model extension:** Generalize the Freund-Witten product to the full $SU(3) \times SU(2) \times U(1)$ gauge group
6. **Cosmological constant:** Explore whether the adelic framework constrains $\Lambda$ through its connection to the zeta function at $s = 4$ (zeta regularization)

### 5.3 Broader Implications

The adelic framework offers a **structural constraint** on physical amplitudes: they must be consistently defined across all completions of $\mathbb{Q}$. This constraint does not determine specific numerical values of fundamental constants, but it constrains their functional relationships — the beta functions, the amplitude structures, the symmetry patterns. In this sense, the adelic framework is a **meta-principle**: it tells us what form physical laws must take, not what specific parameters they must have.

Whether this meta-principle can be extended to yield numerical predictions — through the additional input of compactification geometry — remains the central open question for future work.
