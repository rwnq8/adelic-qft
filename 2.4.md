#### 2.2.3 Statistical Validation

**Bonferroni correction:** With $K$ target ratios and $M = N^2/2$ candidate zero ratios, the significance threshold for a single match is $\alpha_{\text{corrected}} = 0.05 / (K \cdot M)$.

**Null model:** Generate $N$ random numbers uniformly distributed in $[14, 2\pi N/\log N]$ with density matching the zeta zero counting function, and perform the same ratio search. Repeat 1,000 times to establish the expected number of "matches" by chance.

**Hypothesis test:** If the observed number of matches for physical ratios significantly exceeds the null distribution, the connection is statistically meaningful.

|:--------|:---------------|
| No significant matches | Zeta zeros do not encode particle mass ratios |
| Matches at Bonferroni-corrected significance | Genuine number-theoretic connection — possibly via adelic structure of mass generation |
| Matches at uncorrected significance only | Statistical coincidence — multiple comparisons artifact |

## 3. Proposed New Module: M12 — Full $p$-adic Dyson Recursion

### 3.1 Objective

Replace the toy Dyson-like map (M5) with the full $p$-adic hierarchical RG recursion from Lerner & Missarov (1989), computing realistic critical exponents for scalar field theory on $p$-adic trees.

### 3.2 Background

Lerner & Missarov (1989) [1] established that scalar quantum field theory on $p$-adic spaces is equivalent to the hierarchical RG on the Bruhat-Tits tree $T_p$ (the $(p+1)$-regular tree). The RG transformation involves a non-trivial integral recursion:

$$e^{-V_{n+1}(\phi)} = \int_{\mathbb{Q}_p} e^{-V_n(\phi + \psi)} \, d\mu(\psi)$$

where the integration is over $p$-adic spheres and $V_n$ is the effective potential at scale $n$.

### 3.3 Methods

1. **Discretize the potential:** Represent $V(\phi)$ on a mesh of $p$-adic valuations
2. **Implement the spherical integration kernel:** The integral over spheres $S_n$ uses the Haar measure decomposition from M2
3. **Iterate the recursion:** Apply the RG transformation repeatedly, tracking the flow of coupling constants
4. **Extract critical exponents:** Compute $\nu$ (correlation length exponent) and $\eta$ (anomalous dimension) from the linearized RG near the fixed point

### 3.4 Expected Outcomes

- Realistic critical exponents ($\nu \sim 0.6$–$1.0$) for scalar theory on $p$-adic trees
- Comparison of $p$-adic critical exponents with the real-space Ising exponents ($\nu \approx 0.63$)
- Investigation of prime-dependence: do critical exponents depend on $p$?

$$\alpha^{-1} = \frac{V_{T^6}}{(2\pi)^6 \alpha'^3} \cdot \frac{1}{g_s^2}$$

$$\alpha^{-1} \propto \frac{V_{T^6}}{\alpha'^3} \cdot \prod_p g_{s,p}^{-2}$$

## 5. Research Directions for Future Investigation

### 5.1 The Symmetric Point as a Physical Scale

**Question:** Does the symmetric point $a = 0.5$ correspond to a distinguished physical scale?

For the Veneziano amplitude, $a = \alpha(s)$ where $\alpha(s) = \alpha(0) + \alpha' s$ is the Regge trajectory. If $\alpha(s) = 0.5$, then $s = (0.5 - \alpha(0))/\alpha'$. For the $\rho$ trajectory ($\alpha(0) \approx 0.5$), this gives $s \approx 0$ — the $\rho$-meson mass. For the Pomeron trajectory ($\alpha(0) \approx 1.08$), $a = 0.5$ would require negative $s$, corresponding to a spacelike scale.

A systematic investigation of Regge trajectories for the Standard Model particles could identify which trajectories pass through $a = 0.5$ at physically meaningful scales.

### 5.2 Adelic Structure of Electroweak Symmetry Breaking

**Question:** Does the adelic product formula constrain the Higgs potential?

The shape of the Higgs potential $V(\phi) = -\mu^2 \lvert \phi \rvert^2 + \lambda \lvert \phi \rvert^4$ determines the electroweak scale $v = \sqrt{-\mu^2/\lambda}$. If the quartic coupling $\lambda$ has an adelic structure (via the adelic Veneziano amplitude for Higgs scattering), then the electroweak scale might be constrained by adelic consistency.

### 5.3 Langlands Program Connection

**Question:** Is the Freund-Witten Veneziano amplitude a special value of an automorphic L-function?

The functional equation $\xi(s) = \xi(1-s)$ is a special case of the functional equations satisfied by automorphic L-functions in the Langlands program. The adelic Veneziano amplitude $A_\infty \times \prod_p A_p = 1$ similarly satisfies a global functional equation. Investigating whether the Veneziano amplitude can be expressed as a special value of an automorphic L-function on $GL(2)$ would connect the adelic string program to one of the deepest structures in modern number theory.

### 5.4 Cosmological Constant

**Question:** Can the adelic framework constrain $\Lambda$?

The cosmological constant appears in the Einstein-Hilbert action as $\Lambda \int d^4x \sqrt{-g}$. In zeta-function regularization schemes, $\Lambda$ is related to $\zeta(-2) = 0$ and $\zeta(4) = \pi^4/90$. If the adelic framework constrains the zeta function (as it does through the completed zeta $\xi(s)$), it might also constrain $\Lambda$ through the connection between spacetime dimension and zeta regularization.

### 5.5 Adelic Constraints on Gauge Groups

**Question:** Does the adelic product formula select specific gauge groups?

The Freund-Witten formula constrains string amplitudes. In string theory, consistent gauge groups arise from specific worldsheet constructions (heterotic $E_8 \times E_8$, Type I $SO(32)$). If the adelic product formula places additional constraints on the worldsheet CFT, it might restrict the possible gauge groups. This would be a profound result: number theory selecting the gauge group of the Standard Model.
