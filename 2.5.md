## Abstract (150–250 words)

We present a computational investigation of whether the adelic completion of the rational numbers $\mathbb{Q}$ constrains the renormalization group flow of dimensionless physical couplings. The Freund-Witten (1987) adelic product formula for the Veneziano string amplitude, $A_\infty(s,t) \prod_p A_p(s,t) = 1$, is computationally verified using the correct Gel'fand-Graev $p$-adic Gamma function and analytic continuation via the Riemann zeta function. We extract discrete renormalization group maps $f_p(a) = \Gamma_p(a)/\Gamma_\infty(a)$ at each prime, demonstrating systematic prime-dependent structure and convergence to the Archimedean limit as $p \to \infty$. The Archimedean interpolation of these discrete maps yields a beta function with leading-order structure $\beta(\alpha) \propto \alpha^2$, matching the known QED one-loop result in functional form. We find that adelic constraints determine the structure of coupling evolution but not its specific numerical coefficient — that requires additional input from compactification geometry. We also demonstrate that naive adelic products of integrated quantities (partition functions) diverge structurally, clarifying that the product formula constrains multiplicative objects (norms, amplitudes) rather than additive ones. The completed zeta function $\xi(s)$, whose functional equation underlies the Freund-Witten identity, provides the number-theoretic foundation for the adelic framework.

## 1. Introduction & Motivation

The origin of fundamental dimensionless constants — the fine-structure constant $\alpha \approx 1/137$, particle mass ratios, gauge couplings — remains among the deepest mysteries in theoretical physics. The Standard Model accommodates these values as free parameters. A deeper theory should explain them.

One approach is **number-theoretic**: physical laws are expressed over $\mathbb{Q}$, whose structure includes not only the real numbers $\mathbb{R}$ but also the $p$-adic numbers $\mathbb{Q}_p$. Ostrowski's theorem states that these are the only completions. Together they form the **adele ring** $\mathbb{A}_\mathbb{Q}$, governed by the product formula:

$$\lvert q \rvert_\infty \prod_{p} \lvert q \rvert_p = 1 \quad \forall q \in \mathbb{Q}^\times$$

Freund & Witten (1987) showed that the Veneziano string amplitude satisfies an analogous adelic product formula. We computationally verify this result with modern methods, extract its implications for gauge coupling evolution, and critically examine which physical objects are genuinely adelic.

## 3. Discussion

### 3.1 Structure vs. Coefficient

The adelic framework constrains the **structure** of physical laws (functional forms of beta functions, amplitude consistency) but not specific **coefficients** (numerical values of $\alpha$, particle masses). This parallels conformal field theory: conformal symmetry determines correlation function structure, not operator product expansion coefficients.

### 3.2 The Compactification Gap

Predicting $\alpha(M_Z)$ requires mapping $g_s \to \alpha$, which depends on compactification volume, dilaton VEV, and gauge group embedding. Without this data, the adelic framework provides **consistency constraints** but not **numerical predictions**.

### 3.3 Number-Theoretic Foundation

The Freund-Witten product formula is equivalent to the functional equation of $\zeta(s)$. This places adelic string amplitudes in the broader context of the Langlands program, where automorphic L-functions satisfy analogous global identities.

## 4. Conclusion

The adelic framework provides a **structural constraint** on physical amplitudes: they must be consistently defined across all completions of $\mathbb{Q}$. This constraint determines the functional form of coupling evolution but not its specific numerical parameters. The computational verification of the Freund-Witten product formula establishes a solid foundation for further investigation of adelic methods in theoretical physics.

**Key open questions:**
1. Can specific compactification geometries yield numerical predictions for $\alpha$?
2. Does the adelic framework select specific gauge groups?
3. What role does the symmetric point $a = 0.5$ play at physical energy scales?
4. Can the framework be extended to the cosmological constant via zeta regularization?
