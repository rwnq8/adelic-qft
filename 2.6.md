2. **The product formula enforces balance.** If something is small in one place (large $p$-adic norm), it must be large in another. The total product is exactly 1:
   $$\lvert q \rvert_\infty \prod_p \lvert q \rvert_p = 1$$

3. **No runaway infinities.** The only way a ratio becomes infinite in a completion is if the denominator is zero in that completion — but then the numerator would also have to be zero for the product to remain finite. The adelic product formula prevents this.

When those are included, the infinities cancel.

## 1. The Landau Pole: An Archimedean Artifact

### 1.1 The Standard QED Flow

The one-loop QED beta function:

$$\beta(\alpha) \equiv \mu \frac{d\alpha}{d\mu} = \frac{2\alpha^2}{3\pi} \sum_f Q_f^2 = \beta_0 \alpha^2$$

For charged leptons ($e, \mu, \tau$): $\sum_f Q_f^2 = 3$, so $\beta_0 = 2/\pi$.

Integrating:

$$\int \frac{d\alpha}{\alpha^2} = \beta_0 \int d\ln\mu$$

$$-\frac{1}{\alpha(\mu)} + \frac{1}{\alpha(\mu_0)} = \beta_0 \ln\frac{\mu}{\mu_0}$$

$$\alpha(\mu) = \frac{\alpha(\mu_0)}{1 - \beta_0 \alpha(\mu_0) \ln(\mu/\mu_0)}$$

**The Landau pole** occurs when the denominator vanishes:

$$\mu_L = \mu_0 \exp\left(\frac{1}{\beta_0 \alpha(\mu_0)}\right) = m_e \exp\left(\frac{\pi}{2} \cdot 137\right) \approx m_e \cdot e^{215} \approx 10^{93} \; m_e$$

At this scale ($\sim 10^{89}$ GeV), $\alpha \to \infty$ — the coupling diverges. This is far beyond the Planck scale ($\sim 10^{19}$ GeV) and thus not physically relevant for QED alone, but the conceptual problem remains: **the mathematical structure of real QFT predicts its own breakdown.**

### 1.2 Why It's an Artifact

The real number system $\mathbb{R}$ is only ONE of the completions of $\mathbb{Q}$. By Ostrowski's theorem, the full structure of $\mathbb{Q}$ includes all $p$-adic completions $\mathbb{Q}_p$. The Landau pole arises because we have:

1. Restricted to the Archimedean place $\mathbb{R}$ only
2. Ignored the compensating $p$-adic factors
3. Treated $\alpha$ as a real-valued function when it should be an **adele-valued** function

In the adelic framework, the coupling at scale $\mu$ has components at EVERY place:

$$\alpha(\mu) = (\alpha_\infty(\mu), \alpha_2(\mu), \alpha_3(\mu), \alpha_5(\mu), \dots)$$

The product formula constrains the full adelic object. The "divergence" at the real Landau pole is compensated by the $p$-adic components.

### 1.3 The Adelic Boundedness Theorem (Conjecture)

> **Theorem (Adelic Boundedness):** Let $\alpha(\mu)$ be the adelic coupling — a function from the idele group $\mathbb{I}_\mathbb{Q}$ to $\mathbb{A}_\mathbb{Q}^\times$. If $\alpha$ is consistent with the adelic structure (i.e., arises from a well-defined adelic amplitude), then there exists a finite constant $C$ such that:
>
> $$\lvert \alpha_\infty(\mu) \rvert_\infty \cdot \prod_p \lvert \alpha_p(\mu) \rvert_p \leq C < \infty$$
>
> for all $\mu \in \mathbb{I}_\mathbb{Q}$. In particular, $\alpha_\infty(\mu)$ cannot diverge unless compensated by $p$-adic zeros — which the product formula prevents.

**What we can demonstrate computationally:**

## 2. The Adelic RG Flow: Mathematical Structure

### 2.1 Discrete RG Maps from Freund-Witten

The Freund-Witten (1987) adelic Veneziano amplitude provides the bridge:

$$A_\infty(a,b,c) \times \prod_p A_p(a,b,c) = 1$$

where $A_v(a,b,c) = \Gamma_v(a)\Gamma_v(b)\Gamma_v(c)$ and $c = 1 - a - b$.

The **discrete RG maps** at each prime are:

$$f_p(a) = \frac{\Gamma_p(a)}{\Gamma_\infty(a)}$$

These encode how the $p$-adic completion of the coupling differs from the real completion at scale parameter $a$. Key properties:
- $f_p(a) \cdot f_p(1-a) = 1$ (from $\Gamma_p(x)\Gamma_p(1-x) = 1$)
- $f_p(0.5) = 1$ for all $p$ (the symmetric point)
- $f_p(a) \to 1$ as $p \to \infty$ (Archimedean limit)

### 2.2 The Adelic Coupling

The true coupling is the adelic product:

$$\alpha_{\text{adelic}}(a) = \alpha_\infty(a) \times \prod_p F_p(a)$$

where $F_p(a)$ is the $p$-adic correction factor derived from the RG maps.

**At the symmetric point** $a = 0.5$:
- All $\Gamma_v = 1$, all $f_p = 1$
- $\alpha_{\text{adelic}} = \alpha_\infty$
- This is the unification scale where all completions coincide

**Away from the symmetric point:**
- The $p$-adic factors $f_p(a) \neq 1$ provide corrections
- As $a \to 1$ (the real pole), $\Gamma_\infty(a) \to \infty$ while $\Gamma_p(a)$ remains finite
- The full adelic product remains bounded by the analytic continuation via $\zeta(s)$

### 2.3 Analytic Continuation via the Zeta Function

The product over all primes of the Gamma functions has a closed form:

$$\prod_p \Gamma_p(a) = \frac{\zeta(a)}{\zeta(1-a)} = \frac{(2\pi)^a}{2\cos(\pi a/2)\Gamma(a)}$$

This analytic continuation is the mechanism that prevents the Landau pole: the naive, truncated product diverges, but the analytically continued product (which includes ALL completions simultaneously) is finite and well-defined.

The Archimedean normalization is precisely the reciprocal:

$$\Gamma_\infty(a) = \frac{2\cos(\pi a/2)\Gamma(a)}{(2\pi)^a}$$

So that $\Gamma_\infty(a) \times \prod_p \Gamma_p(a) = 1$ exactly.

### 2.4 The Adelic Invariant

The adelic invariant is the quantity that remains constant under the full adelic RG flow. From the Freund-Witten identity:

$$\mathcal{I}(a,b) = A_\infty(a,b,c) \times \prod_p A_p(a,b,c) = 1$$

This is a **kinematic invariant** — it holds for ALL Regge trajectory values $(a,b)$. The invariant is not a single number but the **functional identity itself**: the adelic product equals 1 as a function.

For the physical coupling, the invariant would be:

$$\mathcal{I}_\alpha = \frac{\alpha_{\text{adelic}}(\mu_1)}{\alpha_{\text{adelic}}(\mu_2)}$$

evaluated between two distinguished scales (e.g., the symmetric point and the electron scale). This ratio, being a rational function of adelic objects, should be expressible in terms of zeta values and gamma functions.
