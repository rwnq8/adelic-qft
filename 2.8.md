## 2. Project Overview

### 2.1 The Central Question

> Can the renormalization group flow of dimensionless physical couplings be derived or constrained by the requirement that physical amplitudes are consistently defined across **all** completions of $\mathbb{Q}$?

### 2.2 Ostrowski's Theorem

The only non-trivial completions of $\mathbb{Q}$ are:
- **$\lvert \mathbb{R} \rvert$** (Archimedean, $|\cdot|_\infty$)
- **$\lvert \mathbb{Q}_p \rvert$** for each prime $p$ (non-Archimedean, $|\cdot|_p$)

Together they form the **adele ring** $\mathbb{A}_\mathbb{Q}$. The **adelic product formula**:

$$\lvert q \rvert_\infty \prod_{p} \lvert q \rvert_p = 1 \quad \text{for all } q \in \mathbb{Q}^\times$$

is the only universal constraint relating Archimedean and non-Archimedean measures. If physical laws can be expressed over $\mathbb{Q}$, this constraint must be respected.

### 2.3 The Running-Coupling Correction

The original framing — "can we derive $\alpha \approx 1/137$?" — was the wrong question. $\alpha$ runs with energy: $\alpha(m_e) \approx 1/137.036$, $\alpha(M_Z) \approx 1/127.9$. The correct target is the **beta function**:

$$\beta(\alpha) \equiv \mu \frac{d\alpha}{d\mu}$$

### 2.4 What Makes This Research Distinct

Unlike historical attempts to "derive" $\alpha$ (Eddington's numerology, Wyler's formula, Gilson's approach), this project does not seek a single magic number. It investigates whether the **structure** of physical law — the functional forms of beta functions, coupling trajectories, and amplitude factorizations — is constrained by the requirement of adelic consistency.

`[LLM-INFERRED]` This structural approach is more modest but also more rigorous: it makes claims at the level of mathematical identity (verified) rather than numerical coincidence (unverified).

## 3. Mathematical Results

### 3.1 The Product Formula (M1)

**`[CODE-EXECUTED: test_foundations.py]`**

The adelic product formula $\lvert q \rvert_\infty \cdot \prod_p \lvert q \rvert_p = 1$ was verified for 100+ random rationals with controlled prime factorization. All tests pass at exact `Fraction(1,1)` precision.

$$|q|_\infty = \begin{cases} q & q \geq 0 \\ -q & q < 0 \end{cases}, \quad |q|_p = p^{-v_p(q)}$$

### 3.2 The Freund-Witten Adelic Veneziano Amplitude (M4)

**`[CODE-EXECUTED: gelfand_graev_gamma.py]`**

Freund & Witten (1987) proposed that the Veneziano string amplitude factors adelically:

$$A_\infty(a,b) \cdot \prod_p A_p(a,b) = 1$$

where:
- $A_\infty(a,b) = \Gamma_\infty(a) \cdot \Gamma_\infty(b) \cdot \Gamma_\infty(1-a-b)$
- $\Gamma_\infty(x) = 2\cos(\pi x/2) \cdot \Gamma(x) / (2\pi)^x$ (Archimedean)
- $\Gamma_p(x) = (1 - p^{x-1}) / (1 - p^{-x})$ (Gel'fand-Graev $p$-adic Gamma)
- $A_p(a,b) = \Gamma_p(a) \cdot \Gamma_p(b) \cdot \Gamma_p(1-a-b)$

**Verification results:**

| Identity | Range Tested | Max Error |
|:---------|:-------------|:----------|
| $\Gamma_p(x) \cdot \Gamma_p(1-x) = 1$ | $p \in \{2,3,5,7,11,13,17\}$, $x \in \{0.1,0.3,0.5,0.7,0.9\}$ | $< 10^{-15}$ |
| $\Gamma_\infty(x) \cdot \prod_p \Gamma_p(x) = 1$ | $x \in \{0.1,0.25,0.33,0.5,0.67,0.75,0.9\}$ | $< 10^{-12}$ |
| $A_\infty(a,b) \cdot \prod_p A_p(a,b) = 1$ | 8 $(a,b,c)$ kinematic points | $< 10^{-12}$ |

**Critical implementation detail:** The infinite product over primes converges ONLY via analytic continuation:

$$\prod_p \Gamma_p(x) = \frac{\zeta(x)}{\zeta(1-x)}$$

Naive truncation over finitely many primes **diverges** — at $P=45$ primes, the truncated product exceeds $10^{25}$ for some kinematic points. The equality $= 1$ is a functional identity derived from the Riemann zeta functional equation, not a limit of finite products.

### 3.3 Adelic RG Flow (M11)

**`[CODE-EXECUTED: 2.6.py]`**

The discrete RG map at each prime is:

$$f_p(a) = \frac{\Gamma_p(a)}{\Gamma_\infty(a)}$$

Properties verified:
- **Duality:** $f_p(a) \cdot f_p(1-a) = 1$ exactly (20/20 checks pass)
- **Symmetric point:** $f_p(0.5) = 1$ for all $p$
- **Convergence:** $f_p(a) \to 1$ as $p \to \infty$, but slowly — at $p=97$, $\lvert f_p(0.25) - 1 \rvert = 0.664$

The full adelic coupling is defined as:

$$\alpha_{\text{adelic}}(a) = \alpha_{\text{ref}} \times \frac{\Gamma_\infty(a) \cdot \prod_p \Gamma_p(a)}{\Gamma_\infty(a_{\text{ref}}) \cdot \prod_p \Gamma_p(a_{\text{ref}})}$$

Since $\Gamma_\infty(a) \cdot \prod_p \Gamma_p(a) = 1$ for all $a$, the numerator and denominator both equal $1$, giving:

$$\boxed{\alpha_{\text{adelic}}(a) = \alpha_{\text{ref}} = \text{constant for all } a}$$

The adelic RG flow has **zero running**. The Landau pole — a genuine mathematical divergence in the $\mathbb{R}$-only theory at $\mu_L \approx 10^{90}$ GeV — is eliminated entirely, but so is **all** scale dependence.

### 3.4 What Is and Is Not Adelic

A key structural insight emerged from the failure of M2–M3:

| Object | Adelic Property | Verified? |
|:-------|:----------------|:----------|
| Norms $\lvert q \rvert_v$ | $\prod_v \lvert q \rvert_v = 1$ | ✅ M1 |
| Veneziano amplitude | $\prod_v A_v = 1$ | ✅ M4 |
| Completed zeta $\xi(s)$ | $\xi(s) = \xi(1-s)$ | ✅ M6 |
| Partition function $\Xi$ | $\prod_v Z_v = 1$ | ❌ M3 (diverges to zero) |
| Beta function $B(a,b)$ | $\prod_v B_v = 1$ | ❌ M4-old (diverges) |

> **Central insight:** The adelic product formula constrains **multiplicative** objects (norms, amplitudes, L-functions, zeta factors) — not **additive** ones (partition functions, free energies). This is because the product formula $\prod_v |q|_v = 1$ is a multiplicative identity that integrates to $1$ almost everywhere, but does not extend to sums of exponentials.

## 5. Critical Review: Gaps, Errors, and Tensions

### 5.1 Major Gaps

#### Gap 1: No Bridge to Numerical $\alpha$ (🔴 Critical)

The project's title — "Adelic Constraints on Quantum Field Theory" — implies that the adelic framework unifies or constrains the gauge couplings. The core unfulfilled promise is:

> **The adelic product $= 1$ does not constrain the specific numerical value of $\alpha$ at any scale.**

The synthesis correctly identifies that compactification geometry (mapping the string coupling $g_s$ to the gauge coupling $\alpha$) is needed, but no concrete path is outlined. The M8 beta function reconstruction recovers the $\propto \alpha^2$ functional form but with an undetermined coefficient. This is a **structural result**, not a numerical prediction.

`[LLM-INFERRED]` The gap between "adelic product $= 1$" and "$\alpha(M_Z) \approx 1/127.9$" is larger than acknowledged. The adelic framework may constrain the **functional form** of beta functions (which it does — QED's $\alpha^2$ structure emerges) but leaves their coefficients as free parameters determined by the specific compactification.

#### Gap 2: The Constant-$\alpha$ Tension (🔴 Critical)

The M11 result shows:

$$\alpha_{\text{adelic}}(a) = \alpha_{\text{ref}} = \text{constant for all } a$$

But **physical $\alpha$ runs**: it changes from $1/137.036$ at $m_e$ to $1/127.9$ at $M_Z$ — a 7% variation confirmed by LEP precision measurements. The adelic RG flow, as currently defined, has **zero** running. This is not "constrained running" — it's **no running at all**.

This tension is analyzed in detail in §6.

#### Gap 3: No Standard Model Extension (🟡 Medium)

Only QED ($U(1)$) has been investigated. The gauge group $SU(3) \times SU(2) \times U(1)$ and its adelic structure is untouched. The Freund-Witten amplitude is specifically for the Veneziano model (open string, $U(N)$ gauge group). Extending to the full Standard Model gauge structure is non-trivial.

### 5.2 Methodological Issues

#### Issue 1: Zeta Zero Mass Ratio Search (⚠️ Medium)

**`[CODE-EXECUTED: zeta_analysis.py]`**

The search for mass ratio matches in zeta zero ratios used only 500 zeros and found "exact" matches (W/Z at 0.1% error, top/Higgs at 0.0% error). With $\sim 500 \times 20 = 10,000$ pairs and multiple targets, coincidental matches are statistically expected. No Bonferroni correction or permutation test was applied. These should be flagged as **almost certainly numerological coincidences** until tested with $10^5$+ zeros.

#### Issue 2: Hierarchical RG Critical Exponents (⚠️ Medium)

**`[CODE-EXECUTED: hierarchical_rg.py]`**

For $p \geq 5$, the Dyson map produces **negative** critical exponents $\nu$ (e.g., $\nu_5 \approx -2.08$). A negative $\nu$ means the correlation length diverges as $T \to \infty$, not $T \to T_c$ — physically unusual. `[LLM-INFERRED]` The Dyson map may not be the correct recursion for hierarchical RG on $p$-adic trees. The canonical recursion (Lerner & Missarov 1989) uses a different integral kernel.

#### Issue 3: Missing Null Model Analysis (🟡 Medium)

M9 acknowledges that null models are needed, but the analysis is incomplete. The strongest findings (product $= 1$, zeta functional equation) are **mathematical identities** — they would be "discovered" by any null model that computes Gamma functions and zeta values. A proper null model should randomize the pairing between mathematical structures and physical quantities to determine whether the observed correspondence exceeds chance.

## 6. The Central Tension: Constant $\alpha$ vs. Observed Running

### 6.1 Statement of the Tension

The M11 result establishes:

$$\alpha_{\text{adelic}}(a) = \alpha_{\text{ref}} \quad \text{for all } a$$

where the adelic coupling is defined via the Freund-Witten Gamma product. But physical QED exhibits:

$$\alpha(m_e) = 1/137.036, \quad \alpha(M_Z) = 1/127.934, \quad \Delta \alpha / \alpha \approx 7\%$$

The Landau pole is eliminated, but so is **all** running. This is not a constrained RG flow — it's a **frozen** one.

### 6.2 Three Possible Resolutions

`[LLM-INFERRED]` — The following are theoretical possibilities, not verified results.

#### Resolution A: The Adelic Gamma Product Does Not Describe Gauge Coupling RG

The Freund-Witten product formula applies to **string scattering amplitudes** (the Veneziano amplitude), not to **running gauge couplings**. The mapping from Veneziano Regge trajectories $a(s) = \alpha' s + a_0$ to gauge coupling $\alpha(\mu)$ is non-trivial and involves the specific string compactification. The adelic product $= 1$ may constrain the **string amplitude structure** without directly constraining the low-energy gauge coupling.

**Test:** Construct the adelic structure for the actual QED vertex function $\Gamma_\mu(p,p')$ rather than the Veneziano amplitude.

#### Resolution B: The Beta Function (Not the Coupling) Is the Adelic Object

The definition:

$$\alpha_{\text{adelic}}(a) = \alpha_{\text{ref}} \times \frac{\Gamma_\infty(a) \cdot \prod_p \Gamma_p(a)}{\Gamma_\infty(a_{\text{ref}}) \cdot \prod_p \Gamma_p(a_{\text{ref}})}$$

assumes the coupling evolves by **multiplying** the adelic Gamma product. But in RG theory, the coupling evolves by **integrating the beta function**:

$$\alpha(\mu) = \alpha(\mu_0) + \int_{\mu_0}^\mu \beta(\alpha) \, d\ln\mu$$

The correct adelic object may be the **beta function itself**, not the coupling. The adelic constraint could be:

$$\beta_\infty(\alpha) + \sum_p \beta_p(\alpha) = 0$$

or some multiplicative analog. This would allow non-zero running at each place while maintaining global adelic consistency.

**Test:** Derive $\beta_p(\alpha)$ from the discrete RG map $f_p(a)$ at each prime, and check whether the adelic sum/product of beta functions has special properties.

#### Resolution C: The Observed Running Is the Archimedean Component of a Deeper Structure

Perhaps the physical $\alpha(\mu)$ we observe is $\alpha_\infty(\mu)$ — the Archimedean component of an adelic object. The full adelic coupling includes $p$-adic components $\alpha_p$ that we do not directly measure. The running we observe could be partially canceled by $p$-adic contributions at extreme scales, but at accessible energies ($m_e$ to $M_Z$), the $p$-adic contributions are negligible.

$$\alpha_{\text{observed}}(\mu) = \alpha_\infty(\mu), \quad \alpha_p(\mu) \approx 0 \text{ at low energies}$$

This preserves both the observed running (from the Archimedean component) and the adelic consistency (from the full product).

**Test:** Compute the $p$-adic correction $\delta_p = \alpha_p(M_Z) - \alpha_p(m_e)$ and check whether it's experimentally accessible.

### 6.3 Assessment

`[LLM-INFERRED]` Resolution A is the most parsimonious: the Freund-Witten adelic product applies to string amplitudes, not gauge couplings. The mapping between them is the missing compactification geometry. Resolution B is the most interesting mathematically: it would require constructing an adelic beta function, which could have deep connections to automorphic forms. Resolution C is the most conservative: it acknowledges the observed running while maintaining adelic consistency through unmeasured $p$-adic components.

**The project's principal finding — that the adelic product $= 1$ for string amplitudes — remains correct and significant. The tension arises only when this result is applied to gauge coupling RG flow without the intervening compactification geometry.**
