Module D1 investigated the central tension identified in 2.8.md S6: the M11 finding that $\alpha_{\text{adelic}}(a) = \text{constant}$ (zero running) contradicts the observed 7% running of the fine-structure constant from $m_e$ to $M_Z$.

$$\boxed{\beta_\infty(a) + \sum_p \beta_p(a) = 0 \quad \text{for all } a \in (0,1)}$$

where $\beta_v(a) \equiv \frac{d}{da} \ln \Gamma_v(a)$. This allows $\beta_\infty(a) \neq 0$ (non-zero running) while maintaining adelic consistency through exact cancellation by the sum of $p$-adic beta functions.

**The constant coupling result from M11 came from applying the PRODUCT constraint to $\alpha$ itself. The correct approach applies the SUM (logarithmic derivative) constraint to $\beta(\alpha)$, which naturally allows running.**

## 2. Mathematical Derivation

### 2.1 Place Beta Functions

From the Freund-Witten (1987) Gel'fand-Graev gamma functions:

**Archimedean place:**
$$\Gamma_\infty(a) = \frac{2\cos(\pi a/2)\Gamma(a)}{(2\pi)^a}$$

$$\beta_\infty(a) \equiv \frac{d}{da}\ln\Gamma_\infty(a) = \psi(a) - \ln(2\pi) - \frac{\pi}{2}\tan\left(\frac{\pi a}{2}\right)$$

where $\psi(a) = \Gamma'(a)/\Gamma(a)$ is the digamma function.

**$p$-adic place:**
$$\Gamma_p(a) = \frac{1 - p^{a-1}}{1 - p^{-a}}$$

$$\beta_p(a) \equiv \frac{d}{da}\ln\Gamma_p(a) = -\ln p \cdot \left[\frac{1}{p^{1-a} - 1} + \frac{1}{p^a - 1}\right]$$

**Properties:**
- $\beta_p(0.5) = -\frac{2\ln p}{\sqrt{p} - 1}$ (symmetric point, negative)
- $\beta_p(a) + \beta_p(1-a) = 0$ (antisymmetry under $a \leftrightarrow 1-a$)
- All $\beta_p(a) < 0$ for $a \in (0,1)$ — each prime contributes a downward push

### 2.2 Analytic Sum Over All Primes

The sum over all primes has a closed form via analytic continuation:

$$\prod_p \Gamma_p(a) = \frac{\zeta(a)}{\zeta(1-a)}$$

Taking the logarithmic derivative:

$$\sum_p \beta_p(a) = \frac{d}{da} \ln \frac{\zeta(a)}{\zeta(1-a)} = \frac{\zeta'(a)}{\zeta(a)} + \frac{\zeta'(1-a)}{\zeta(1-a)}$$

### 2.3 The Adelic Beta Constraint

Differentiating the Freund-Witten identity:

$$\frac{d}{da} \ln \left[\Gamma_\infty(a) \cdot \prod_p \Gamma_p(a)\right] = \frac{d}{da} \ln(1) = 0$$

$$\beta_\infty(a) + \sum_p \beta_p(a) = 0$$

Equivalently:

$$\psi(a) - \ln(2\pi) - \frac{\pi}{2}\tan\left(\frac{\pi a}{2}\right) + \frac{\zeta'(a)}{\zeta(a)} + \frac{\zeta'(1-a)}{\zeta(1-a)} = 0$$

This is the **differential form of the Freund-Witten identity**.

### 4.2 Why the Constant Coupling Result Was Wrong

The M11 definition:

$$\alpha_{\text{adelic}}(a) = \alpha_{\text{ref}} \times \frac{\Gamma_\infty(a) \cdot \prod_p \Gamma_p(a)}{\Gamma_\infty(a_{\text{ref}}) \cdot \prod_p \Gamma_p(a_{\text{ref}})}$$

Since $\Gamma_\infty(a) \cdot \prod_p \Gamma_p(a) = 1$ for all $a$, the numerator and denominator both equal 1, forcing $\alpha_{\text{adelic}}(a) = \alpha_{\text{ref}}$ (constant). This corresponds to applying the **product** constraint to the coupling.

**The correct approach:** The RG flow is governed by the beta function, not by multiplying Gamma functions. The adelic constraint on the beta function comes from the **logarithmic derivative** of the product identity — giving the **sum** constraint $\beta_\infty + \sum_p \beta_p = 0$.

### 4.4 Three-Tier Physical Picture

1. **Archimedean ($\beta_\infty$):** The beta function we observe in particle physics — governs the running we measure at colliders ($\alpha$ from $1/137$ to $1/128$).

2. **$p$-adic ($\beta_p$ for each $p$):** The compensating beta functions at each prime — individually unobservable at low energies but ensuring global adelic consistency.

3. **Adelic constraint ($\sum = 0$):** The sum of all place-beta-functions is identically zero — a mathematical identity derived from the Freund-Witten product formula. This does not force $\alpha$ to be constant; it forces the **total logarithmic derivative** to vanish.
