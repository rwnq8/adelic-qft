## 1. Executive Summary

Module R10 investigates whether the adelic structure (Freund-Witten product formula, beta constraint) extends from QED ($U(1)$) to the full Standard Model gauge group $SU(3) \times SU(2) \times U(1)$.

**Principal finding:** The adelic structure constrains the **functional form** of beta functions ($\beta \sim \alpha^2$ at one loop) but not their specific **rational coefficients** ($b_1 = 41/10$, $b_2 = -19/6$, $b_3 = -7$). These coefficients are determined by the particle content (group theory factors) and the compactification geometry. The SM beta coefficients do not directly match known adelic/zeta values — they encode the specific matter content of the Standard Model.

## 2. Standard Model Beta Functions

The one-loop beta functions in the MS-bar scheme:

| Gauge Group | Coefficient $b_i$ | Value |
|:-----------|:---:|:---:|
| $U(1)$ (GUT normalized) | $b_1$ | $41/10 = 4.1$ |
| $SU(2)$ | $b_2$ | $-19/6 \approx -3.167$ |
| $SU(3)$ | $b_3$ | $-7$ |

**Ratios:** $b_1/b_2 = -1.295$, $b_2/b_3 = 0.452$, $b_1/b_3 = -0.586$

The $SU(2)$ and $SU(3)$ coefficients are negative (asymptotic freedom), while $U(1)$ is positive (Landau pole). This matches the Veneziano amplitude's UV behavior: $\beta_\infty < 0$ for all $a$ — asymptotic freedom in the Veneziano sector, opposite to QED.

## 4. GUT Unification

One-loop unification in the SM:

- $M_{\text{GUT}}(1-2) = 2.17 \times 10^{13}$ GeV (from $U(1)$–$SU(2)$ intersection)
- $M_{\text{GUT}}(2-3) = 1.28 \times 10^{16}$ GeV (from $SU(2)$–$SU(3)$ intersection)
- **Ratio = 590** — exact unification does NOT occur in the SM (requires SUSY)

The adelic structure might select specific matter content that enables unification, but this requires specifying the compactification geometry (M13).

## 5. The Adelic Unification Hypothesis

A true "adelic unification" would require:

1. **Automorphic L-function** (D4): Specifying which automorphic representation corresponds to the SM gauge group
2. **Compactification geometry** (M13): Determining the Calabi-Yau manifold that maps the Veneziano amplitude to SM gauge couplings
3. **Beta coefficients**: Computing $b_1, b_2, b_3$ from the compactification and comparing with experiment

The adelic structure constrains the **functional form** of beta functions ($\beta \sim \alpha^2$ at one loop) but not the specific rational coefficients. These are determined by the matter content of the compactification — a problem for future work.
