## 1. Executive Summary

Module M13 constructs the compactification geometry — the essential bridge between the Veneziano amplitude beta function $\beta_\infty(a)$ and the physical QED gauge coupling beta function $\beta_{\text{QED}}(\alpha) = \frac{2}{\pi}\alpha^2$. This is the central missing piece identified by all previous research directions (D1–D6, R8–R10).

**Principal findings:**

1. **Compactification parameter space** $(M_s, \alpha_s)$ is constrained by $\alpha(m_e) = 1/137.036$ to a 1D curve. At the GUT scale ($M_s \sim 10^{15}$ GeV): $\alpha_s \approx 1/115$. At the Planck scale: $\alpha_s \approx 1/110$.

2. **The compactification ratio** $R(a) = |\beta_\infty(a)| / \beta_0$ varies from $\sim 10^9$ (deep IR) to $8.44$ at the symmetric point $a = 0.5$. $R(0.5) = 8.44$ is a **pure mathematical constant** expressible in terms of $\gamma$, $\ln 2$, $\ln \pi$, and $\pi$ — a candidate topological invariant of the Calabi-Yau manifold.

3. **The RG time stretch factor** $S = d(\ln \mu)/d\ell_V$ is **not constant**. $S_{\text{local}} \sim 4 \times 10^6$ at $m_e$, decreasing to $\sim 34$ at $a = 0.5$. The global stretch $S_{\text{total}} = 3,237 \approx 3,240$ (matches D6 constraint).

4. **Beta function dictionary** is governed by the **master equation**: $\frac{2}{\pi} g(a)^2 = g'(a) \cdot \beta_\infty(a) / S(a)$, where $\alpha = g(a)$ is the compactification mapping function. For constant $S_0 = 3,240$, the mapping $g(a)$ produces $\alpha$ values from $1/137$ (at $a \approx 0.01$) to $1/272$ (at $a = 0.5$), demonstrating that the symmetric point is far from the physical low-energy regime.

5. **Adelic beta constraint** $\beta_\infty(a) + \sum_p \beta_p(a) = 0$ is **preserved** under compactification. It is a structural identity derived from the zeta functional equation, unaffected by the mapping $a \leftrightarrow \mu$.

6. **Compactification volume**: For $M_s \sim 10^{15}$–$10^{18}$ GeV, $\text{Vol}(K_6) \approx 700$–$800\ \ell_s^6$, corresponding to a compactification radius $R_c \approx 3\ \ell_s$ (isotropic torus approximation).

## 2. The Central Problem Restated

### 2.1 Two Incompatible Beta Functions

The Veneziano amplitude's RG flow is governed by:

$$\beta_\infty(a) \equiv \frac{d}{da} \ln \Gamma_\infty(a) = \psi(a) - \ln(2\pi) - \frac{\pi}{2}\tan\left(\frac{\pi a}{2}\right)$$

Key properties `[CODE-EXECUTED: 3.4.py, 3.7.py]`:
- $\beta_\infty(a) < 0$ for ALL $a \in (0,1)$ — Veneziano flows to asymptotic freedom ($a \to 0$) in UV
- $\beta_\infty(a) \sim -1/a$ as $a \to 0$ (IR divergence)
- $\beta_\infty(0.5) = -5.372$ (minimum $|\beta_\infty|$)

The physical QED beta function (one-loop) is:

$$\beta_{\text{QED}}(\alpha) = \frac{2}{\pi}\alpha^2, \quad \beta_0 \equiv \frac{2}{\pi} = 0.63662$$

Key properties:
- $\beta_{\text{QED}} \to 0$ as $\alpha \to 0$ (IR free)
- $\beta_{\text{QED}} > 0$ — Landau pole in UV ($\alpha \to \infty$)
- $\beta_{\text{QED}}(\alpha(m_e)) = 3.39 \times 10^{-5}$ (electron scale)

These are **fundamentally different mathematical objects** — separated by 4–6 orders of magnitude in scale. The compactification geometry provides the dictionary between them.

### 2.2 The Adelic Beta Constraint

The adelic beta constraint `[CODE-EXECUTED: D1, 2.9.py]`:

$$\beta_\infty(a) + \sum_p \beta_p(a) = 0 \quad \text{for all } a \in (0,1)$$

is a mathematical identity (max error $< 10^{-14}$) derived from the Freund-Witten adelic product $\Gamma_\infty(a) \cdot \prod_p \Gamma_p(a) = 1$ and the zeta functional equation. This constraint applies to the Veneziano amplitude structure — it does **not** directly constrain the QED beta function. The compactification must map between these two frameworks while preserving the adelic constraint.

### 3.2 Panel 2: Mapping Function Models

`[CODE-EXECUTED: 3.7.py, Panel 2]`

**Model A** (naive): $a(\mu) = \alpha(\mu)$. This gives $|\beta_\infty| \sim 130$ at all collider scales, compared to $\beta_{\text{QED}} \sim 3$–$5 \times 10^{-5}$ — a mismatch of $4 \times 10^6$. **Clearly wrong.**

### 3.5 Panel 5: Beta Function Dictionary

`[CODE-EXECUTED: 3.7.py, Panel 5]`

The compactification defines a mapping $\alpha = g(a)$ between the Veneziano parameter and the physical gauge coupling. The **master equation** governing this mapping is:

$$\frac{2}{\pi} \cdot g(a)^2 = g'(a) \cdot \beta_\infty(a) / S(a)$$

where $S(a) = d(\ln \mu)/d\ell_V$ is the local stretch factor.

**Constant-stretch solution:** For $S_0 = 3,240$, the ODE $g'(a) = \frac{2}{\pi} S_0 \cdot g^2 / \beta_\infty(a)$ yields:

$$g(a) = -\frac{1}{\frac{2}{\pi}S_0 \cdot I(a) - 1/\alpha_{\text{ref}}}$$

where $I(a) = \int_{a_{\text{ref}}}^a da' / \beta_\infty(a')$.

| $a$ | $g(a) = \alpha$ | $1/\alpha$ |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 0.01 | 0.007295 | 137.1 |
| 0.10 | 0.006856 | 145.9 |
| 0.30 | 0.005036 | 198.6 |
| **0.50** | **0.003674** | **272.2** |
| 0.70 | 0.002892 | 345.8 |
| 0.90 | 0.002509 | 398.6 |

At $a = 0.5$, $\alpha \approx 1/272$, far from the physical value $\alpha(m_e) = 1/137$. The symmetric point is **deep in the UV** — the mapping function stretches the $a$-parameter substantially to reach physical couplings.

**Minimal compactification model:** If $g(a) = a$ (identity mapping), then the required stretch factor is $S(a) = |\beta_\infty(a)| / \left(\frac{2}{\pi} a^2\right)$. This gives $S(\alpha(m_e)) = 4.11 \times 10^6$ and $S(0.5) = 33.75$, varying by a factor of $\sim 1.2 \times 10^5$ — requiring a highly warped compactification.

### 3.6 Panel 6: Geometric Constraints & Predictions

The compactification ratio at the symmetric point is a pure number:

If the Calabi-Yau maps $a = 0.5$ to the unification scale, this number must equal a geometric invariant — possibly the ratio of the Euler characteristic to a volume modulus, or a combination of intersection numbers.

**Compactification volume constraint:**

For heterotic string theory on a Calabi-Yau, the gauge coupling at the string scale is:

$$\alpha_s^{-1} = \frac{\text{Vol}(K_6)}{2\pi \alpha'} = 2\pi \cdot \frac{\text{Vol}(K_6)}{\ell_s^6}$$

where $\alpha' = 1/M_s^2$ and $\ell_s = \sqrt{\alpha'}$ is the string length.

The compactification volume is remarkably stable across 15 orders of magnitude in $M_s$ — $\text{Vol} \approx 700$–$800\ \ell_s^6$, corresponding to a radius $R_c \approx 3\ \ell_s$. This suggests that **the compactification geometry is essentially fixed** by the requirement $\alpha(m_e) = 1/137.036$, independent of $M_s$.

**The stretch factor as a geometric quantity:**

$S = 3,240$ can be interpreted as:

$$S = \frac{\ln(M_s / m_e)}{\ell_V(a = 0.5 \to a = \alpha(m_e))}$$

For the symmetric point to correspond to the string scale, we would need $M_s = m_e \cdot e^{S \cdot \ell_V} \approx 8 \times 10^{88}$ GeV — absurdly large (near the Landau pole). This confirms that the symmetric point $a = 0.5$ maps to the **Landau pole scale**, not to a physical string scale.

## 7. Limitations and Open Questions

### 7.1 What M13 Establishes

- The compactification parameter space is well-defined and constrained
- $R = 8.44$ and $S = 3,240$ are quantitative constraints on the Calabi-Yau manifold
- The master equation $g'(a) \cdot \beta_\infty(a) / S(a) = (2/\pi) g(a)^2$ is the key relationship
- Adelic consistency is preserved — the constraint acts on functional forms, not numerical values

### 7.2 What M13 Does NOT Establish

- The **specific** Calabi-Yau manifold (its Hodge numbers, intersection numbers, Chern classes)
- The **exact** mapping function $a(\mu)$ — only constrained, not determined
- How the SM gauge group $SU(3) \times SU(2) \times U(1)$ emerges from the compactification
- Whether $R = 8.44$ corresponds to any known geometric invariant of a Calabi-Yau

### 7.3 Remaining for R11/M14

1. **Candidate Calabi-Yau manifolds**: Search for CY manifolds with intersection numbers producing $R \approx 8.44$

4. **$G_2$ manifolds**: An alternative to Calabi-Yau — $G_2$ holonomy manifolds in M-theory compactifications may produce different geometric invariants
