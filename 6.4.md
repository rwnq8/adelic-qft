### 3.4 Beta Function Relations (H4) — NO CLEAR PATTERN

No quark analogue of $\log(m_\mu/m_e) \approx 16/3$ was found. The QCD beta function has a fundamentally different structure:
- **QED:** $b_0 \propto \sum_f Q_f^2$ — pure fermion contribution
- **QCD:** $b_0 \propto 11N_c - 2n_f$ — gluon loops dominate

The running of $n_f$ across quark thresholds further complicates any fixed relation. [LLM-INFERRED]

### 4.1 Why Thrust H Is Inconclusive, Not Failed

Thrust H differs from Thrusts F and G because the experimental uncertainties ARE the limiting factor, not the theory:
- Lepton masses are known to $\sim 10^{-9}$ relative precision — the $3 \times 10^{-4}$ deviation from $16/3$ is $65,000\sigma$, ruling out the coincidence definitively
- Light quark masses are known to $\sim 25\%$ relative precision — almost any rational number within a factor of 2 would be "consistent with experiment"

The quark sector CANNOT currently distinguish between "the adelic framework makes a prediction" and "a random rational number happens to fall within the error bar."

### 4.2 The One Interesting Finding

$\lvert V_{us} \rvert \approx \sqrt{m_d/m_s}$ works to $0.3\%$ — far better than quark mass uncertainties would suggest. This is a known phenomenological relation (Fritzsch texture, 1970s). Whether it has an adelic/zeta origin or is a separate phenomenon cannot be determined with current precision.
