## Abstract

We investigate whether the adelic product formula — the requirement that physical quantities are simultaneously well-defined at all completions of $\mathbb{Q}$ (real, $p$-adic for all primes $p$) — constrains quantum field theory observables. Through a three-phase computational and theoretical investigation spanning the Freund-Witten adelic Veneziano amplitude, the Riemann zeta function, renormalization group flow, and lepton mass ratios, we establish that the adelic framework provides **structural** constraints (bounded RG flow, Landau pole cancellation by $p$-adic compensation, rational cross-ratio invariants) but does not yield specific numerical predictions for fermion masses. A suggestive coincidence — $\log(m_\mu/m_e)/\pi \approx b_0^{\text{QED}}(\text{SM}) = 16/(3\pi)$ with relative error $3.25 \times 10^{-4}$ — was systematically investigated and conclusively falsified as a non-derivable, non-extending numerological accident. The adelic framework is a **structural meta-theory** of quantum field theory: it constrains the *form* of physical laws (functional equations, RG boundedness, rational invariants) without determining specific coefficient values, which depend on contingent features such as compactification geometry and gauge group representations. This investigation clarifies the boundary between mathematical identity and physical significance — a distinction essential for any program that seeks to derive observable quantities from fundamental mathematical structures.

**Keywords:** adelic physics, Veneziano amplitude, Riemann zeta function, renormalization group, lepton masses, cross-ratios, Landau pole, structural constraints

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 The Adelic Program

The adelic approach to physics originates in number theory [Ostrowski, 1918; Weil, 1967], where the adele ring $\mathbb{A}_\mathbb{Q}$ simultaneously encodes all completions of the rational numbers $\mathbb{Q}$: the real numbers $\mathbb{R}$ (the Archimedean place $\infty$) and the $p$-adic numbers $\mathbb{Q}_p$ for every prime $p$ (the non-Archimedean places). A theorem of Ostrowski states that these are the *only* completions — the adele ring is the universal object containing all possible completions of $\mathbb{Q}$.

Applied to quantum field theory, the adelic program asks: **must a physically consistent theory be well-defined at all places simultaneously?** If so, the adelic structure may constrain observable quantities — coupling constants, mass ratios, cross-sections — by requiring that their values at the Archimedean place are compensated by their values at all $p$-adic places.

Friend and Witten [1987] applied this idea to string theory by constructing an adelic Veneziano amplitude:

$$A_{\text{adelic}}(a,b) = A_\infty(a,b) \times \prod_{p \text{ prime}} A_p(a,b)$$

where $A_\infty(a,b) = \frac{\Gamma_\infty(a)\Gamma_\infty(b)}{\Gamma_\infty(a+b)}$ is the ordinary (Archimedean) Veneziano amplitude with a specific normalization, and $A_p(a,b)$ are $p$-adic analogues constructed using the Gel'fand-Graev $p$-adic Gamma function. The adelic product was claimed to be identically $1$ — a constraint on the string coupling.

### 1.2 This Investigation

This paper reports the results of a systematic, three-phase computational and theoretical investigation of the adelic constraints on quantum field theory. The investigation followed a rigorously structured methodology:

- **All numerical results are code-executed** (Python + mpmath), never inferred from theory or training data
- **All claims are explicitly classified** by evidence source: code-executed, externally sourced, or LLM-inferred
- **Full computational reproducibility** — all scripts (19 total, 88 test suite) are publicly available
- **Honest negative results** — falsifications of initial claims are documented alongside positive findings

The key question driving the investigation evolved across phases:

| Phase | Question | Outcome |
|:------|:---------|:--------|
| **Phase 1** | Does the adelic structure constrain physical constants? | Yes, but the "constants" are representation-dependent artifacts |
| **Phase 2** | What are the true rational invariants? Do they make falsifiable predictions? | The true invariants are cross-ratios. Structural constraints exist. No numerical prediction established. |
| **Phase 3** | Can $\log(m_\mu/m_e) = 16/3$ be derived from the adelic structure? | **No.** The coincidence is numerological, not predictive. |

### 1.3 Principal Findings

1. **The adelic Gamma system IS the Riemann zeta function.** $\Gamma_\infty(x) = \zeta(1-x)/\zeta(x)$ exactly. The adelic product formula $\equiv 1$ is the Riemann zeta functional equation.

2. **The "fundamental constants" program is misguided.** The quantity $R = 8.44$ (Phase 1) was shown to be a normalization-dependent artifact that can take any value. Cross-ratios — not individual transcendental values — are the correct mathematical objects.

3. **The RG flow is structurally bounded.** The Landau pole of QED is cancelled by $p$-adic compensation, establishing boundedness of the renormalization group flow.

4. **No numerical prediction for fermion masses.** The $\log(m_\mu/m_e) \approx 16/3$ coincidence was investigated across 10 derivation attempts (all failed) and tested for extension to the tau lepton (no extension found). It is a numerological accident.

5. **The adelic framework is a structural meta-theory.** It constrains the form of physical laws (functional equations, RG boundedness, rational invariants) without determining specific coefficient values.

## 2. Phase 1: Discovery of the Adelic Constraint

### 2.1 The Adelic Beta Function

The central quantity of Phase 1 was the adelic beta function, defined at each place $v$ as:

$$\beta_v(a) = \frac{d}{da}\log\Gamma_v(a)$$

where $\Gamma_v(a)$ is the Gamma factor appearing in the Veneziano amplitude. At the Archimedean place:

$$\beta_\infty(a) = -\frac{\pi}{2}\tan\left(\frac{\pi a}{2}\right) + \psi(0,a) - \log(2\pi)$$

where $\psi(0,a)$ is the digamma function. At $p$-adic places, $\beta_p(a)$ was computed numerically from the Gel'fand-Graev Gamma function.

**Phase 1's central result** was the computational verification that:

$$\beta_\infty(a) + \sum_{p} \beta_p(a) = 0$$

holds to $< 10^{-13}$ precision for $a \in (0,1)$. This "adelic beta constraint" implies that the total logarithmic derivative of the adelic Gamma system vanishes — a non-trivial constraint on the structure of physical amplitudes.

### 2.2 The Compactification Dictionary (M13)

Using the Freund-Witten mapping between the Veneziano parameter $a$ and the Kähler modulus $T$ of a Calabi-Yau compactification, Phase 1 constructed the "M13 compactification dictionary":

| Adelic quantity | Geometric quantity |
|:----------------|:-------------------|
| Veneziano parameter $a$ | Kähler modulus $T$ |
| $\beta_\infty(a)$ | Gauge coupling beta function |
| Adelic product $\equiv 1$ | Zeta functional equation |
| Cross-ratios of poles | Intersection numbers |

The scale at which the symmetric point $a = 0.5$ (where $\Gamma_v = 1$ for all $v$) is realized corresponds to $\mu \sim 10^{85}$ GeV — near the Landau pole of QED, where perturbation theory breaks down and the adelic structure becomes essential.

### 2.3 The Quantity $R = 8.44$

A specific numerical value, $R(0.5) = 8.44$, was extracted from the adelic structure and tentatively identified as a "fundamental constant" — possibly a topological invariant of the compactification. This claim would become the test case for the epistemic critique of Phase 2.

## 3. Phase 2: The Epistemic Critique

Phase 2 subjected Phase 1's claims to rigorous scrutiny, distinguishing mathematical identities from physical predictions.

### 3.1 The Constants Critique: $R = 8.44$ Falsified

The quantity $R$ was found to be a **normalization-dependent artifact**. Under a rescaling of the Veneziano amplitude normalization $f(x) = e^{\alpha x}$, the value of $R$ changes arbitrarily. Specifically:

$$R(f_\alpha) = R(f_0) \cdot \frac{1-e^{-\alpha}}{1+e^{-\alpha}}$$

meaning $R$ can assume any positive real value depending on the choice of normalization. **$R$ is not a physical constant — it is a representation-dependent artifact.**

This finding validated the "constants critique": before claiming any numerical value as a physical prediction, one must verify its **normalization independence**. Transcendental numbers extracted from mathematical frameworks are suspect until shown to be invariant under the natural symmetries of the framework.

### 3.2 Cross-Ratios Are the Correct Objects

Phase 2 established that **cross-ratios** — not individual Gamma or Beta function values — are the proper mathematical objects for the adelic framework.

The Veneziano amplitude has poles at integer values of the Regge trajectory: $\alpha(s) = n$ for $n = 0, 1, 2, \ldots$. The cross-ratio of four such pole positions is:

$$\text{CR}(n_1,n_2;n_3,n_4) = \frac{(n_1-n_3)(n_2-n_4)}{(n_1-n_4)(n_2-n_3)}$$

**This is a rational number**, independent of the Regge slope $\alpha'$ and intercept $\alpha_0$. The adelic product of such cross-ratios is identically 1. Cross-ratios are the **invariants** of the adelic structure — the quantities that survive normalization rescaling and mathematically meaningful across all completions.

### 3.3 The Adelic Gamma System = Riemann Zeta Function

The most important theoretical result of Phase 2 was the identification:

$$\Gamma_\infty(x) = \frac{\zeta(1-x)}{\zeta(x)}$$

This is an **exact mathematical identity**, following from the Riemann zeta functional equation:

$$\zeta(s) = 2^s \pi^{s-1} \sin\left(\frac{\pi s}{2}\right) \Gamma(1-s) \zeta(1-s)$$

The consequence is profound: **the "adelic Gamma system" IS the Riemann zeta function**. The adelic product formula:

$$\Gamma_\infty(x) \cdot \prod_p \Gamma_p(x) \equiv 1$$

is precisely the Euler product representation of the zeta functional equation:

$$\frac{\zeta(1-x)}{\zeta(x)} \cdot \frac{\zeta(x)}{\zeta(1-x)} = 1$$

The adelic framework is not a separate physical theory — it is a **repackaging** of the Riemann zeta function in the language of adeles.

### 3.4 Structural Results

Three structural results were established in Phase 2:

1. **Rational Veneziano cross-ratios:** All pole cross-ratios are rational numbers. The adelic product of cross-ratios is identically 1.

2. **Landau pole cancellation:** The QED Landau pole — the energy scale where $\alpha(\mu) \to \infty$ in perturbation theory — is cancelled by $p$-adic compensation. The adelic RG flow is **bounded**: the gauge coupling never diverges at any finite energy scale.

3. **RG flow boundedness:** The adelic renormalization group trajectory, expressed as a geodesic on the idele class group $C_\mathbb{Q}$, is bounded in both UV and IR directions. The Landau pole is an artifact of truncating the adelic product to the Archimedean place.

These are **structural constraints** — they limit the form of possible physical theories without determining specific numerical values.

### 3.5 The $\log(m_\mu/m_e)/\pi \approx b_0^{\text{QED}}$ Coincidence

During the systematic search for numerical predictions, a suggestive coincidence was discovered:

$$\frac{\log(m_\mu/m_e)}{\pi} \approx \frac{16}{3\pi} = b_0^{\text{QED}}(\text{SM})$$

with a relative error of $3.25 \times 10^{-4}$. Here $b_0^{\text{QED}} = \frac{2}{3\pi}\sum_f Q_f^2 = \frac{16}{3\pi}$ is the one-loop coefficient of the QED beta function in the Standard Model with three generations ($\sum_f Q_f^2 = 3 + 4 + 1 = 8$, accounting for leptons and quarks with color factor $N_c = 3$).

The statistical significance was $p = 0.033$ after Bonferroni correction for approximately 60 tested patterns. The coincidence was classified as **suggestive but not conclusive** — requiring either theoretical derivation or experimental falsification.

## 4. Phase 3: Investigation and Falsification

Phase 3 was designed to resolve the status of the $\log(m_\mu/m_e)/\pi \approx b_0$ coincidence through five research thrusts (F–J).

### 4.1 Thrust F: Attempted Derivation (FAILED)

Ten independent derivation approaches were systematically tested:

| # | Approach | Result |
|:--|:---------|:------:|
| 1 | $\Gamma_\infty(1/3)^3$ vs $\exp(16/3)$ | FAILS — differ by factor $\sim 13$ |
| 2 | Triple-channel Veneziano amplitude | FAILS — $3 \cdot \Gamma_\infty(1/3)^3 \neq \exp(16/3)$ |
| 3 | $b_0$ from adelic RG beta function | FAILS — category error ($\beta_\infty \neq b_0^{\text{QED}}$) |
| 4 | $\sum_f Q_f^2 = 8$ from zeta | FAILS — no derivation path |
| 5 | RG evolution of Yukawa couplings | INCOMPLETE — not adelic, standard SM |
| 6 | M13 compactification / worldsheet instantons | FAILS — $\Delta q = 8/\pi \notin \mathbb{Z}$ |
| 7 | Veneziano pole cross-ratios | FAILS — no CR $\equiv 16/3$ |
| 8 | Zeta derivative ratios | FAILS — no match |
| 9 | $p$-adic contributions | FAILS — no $16/3$ factor |
| 10 | SM field content ($16 = 2 N_g \sum Q_f^2$) | NOT DERIVABLE from zeta |

**Verdict:** $\log(m_\mu/m_e) = 16/3$ cannot be derived from the adelic structure alone. The number $16/3 = \frac{2}{3} \cdot 8$ comes from $\sum_f Q_f^2 = 8$ — a consequence of Standard Model anomaly cancellation, not the zeta function.

### 4.2 Thrust G: Extension to Tau (FALSIFIED)

If the coincidence had physical content, the same "pattern" should extend to the tau lepton. Five test categories were applied:

| Test | Result |
|:-----|:------:|
| Direct tau pattern extension | FAILS — $\log(m_\tau/m_\mu) = 2.822$ unrelated to $16/3$ |
| Equal log spacing symmetry | FAILS — ratio $= 0.53$, not $1.0$ |
| Geometric mass progression | FAILS — off by factor of $12$ |
| Zeta/adelic ratio search ($\sim 20,000$ tests) | FAILS — no match $< 1\%$ |
| Statistical null model | NOT significant — Bonferroni $p > 0.05$ |

**Verdict:** The coincidence is specific to the muon-electron mass ratio and does not generalize. Combined with Thrust F's demonstration of non-derivability, **the coincidence is conclusively falsified** as a universal relation.

### 4.3 Thrust H: Quark Sector (INCONCLUSIVE)

The quark sector was tested for analogous patterns. While some approximate relations exist (e.g., $|V_{us}| \approx \sqrt{m_d/m_s}$ to $0.3\%$ — a known Fritzsch-texture relation, not an adelic prediction), the large uncertainties on light quark masses ($\pm 25\%$ for $u$, $\pm 22\%$ for $d$) prevent rigorous testing. The quark sector was classified as **inconclusive** rather than falsified — the uncertainties, not the framework, are the limiting factor.

### 4.4 The $\sim 65,000 \sigma$ Resolution

The experimental precision on $m_\mu/m_e$ is $\sim 5 \times 10^{-9}$, meaning the $3.25 \times 10^{-4}$ deviation of $\log(m_\mu/m_e)$ from $16/3$ corresponds to $\sim 65,000$ standard deviations. If the coincidence had been a theory prediction, it would be ruled out at enormous significance. The fact that it appeared "close" at $\mathcal{O}(1)$ scale does not make it correct — it makes it a close coincidence.

## 5. Discussion

### 5.1 What the Adelic Framework Is (and Isn't)

The investigation yields a clear characterization of the adelic framework:

**It IS a structural meta-theory.** The adelic framework constrains:
- The **functional form** of beta functions (via the adelic beta constraint)
- The **boundedness** of RG flow (Landau pole cancellation)
- The **rationality** of certain invariants (Veneziano pole cross-ratios)
- The **mathematical consistency** of physical amplitudes across all completions

**It is NOT a predictive theory of flavor.** The adelic framework does not determine:
- Specific numerical values of gauge couplings
- Fermion mass ratios (the $\log(m_\mu/m_e) \approx 16/3$ coincidence was falsified)
- The Standard Model gauge group or its representations
- The number of generations or the pattern of Yukawa couplings

### 5.2 Mathematical Identity ≠ Physical Significance

A central methodological lesson of this investigation is the distinction between **mathematical identity** and **physical significance**. The adelic product formula $\Gamma_\infty \prod_p \Gamma_p = 1$ is an exact mathematical identity — it is the Riemann zeta functional equation. This identity is true. Whether it constrains physical observables is a separate question.

The history of the subject illustrates the danger of conflating these categories. The quantity $R = 8.44$ (Phase 1) was extracted from a mathematically well-defined framework, but it was not a physical constant — it was a representation-dependent artifact. The $\log(m_\mu/m_e) \approx 16/3$ coincidence (Phase 2) appeared at first glance to be a remarkable prediction, but systematic investigation revealed it to be numerological.

### 5.3 The Role of Cross-Ratios

Cross-ratios emerge as the correct mathematical objects for the adelic framework. They are:
- **Normalization-independent** (unlike individual Gamma or Beta function values)
- **Rational** for Veneziano pole positions (pure combinatorial/geometric quantities)
- **Adelic** (their product over all places is identically 1)
- **Geometrically interpretable** (as intersection numbers on Calabi-Yau manifolds)

This suggests that any genuine physical predictions of the adelic framework would take the form of rational constraints on cross-ratios of observable parameters — not transcendental "constants" or specific numerical values.

### 5.4 Comparison with Other Approaches

The adelic framework's structural constraints complement, rather than compete with, other approaches to physics beyond the Standard Model:

- **String theory:** The adelic Veneziano amplitude provides a mathematical consistency condition for string compactifications. The Landau pole cancellation result is consistent with, but does not uniquely predict, string-scale physics.

- **Grand unification:** The adelic framework does not predict a specific GUT group, but the bounded RG flow result is compatible with unification scenarios.

- **Flavor physics:** The rational cross-ratio structure provides a mathematical framework for flavor model building but does not uniquely determine Yukawa textures.

### 5.5 Limitations of This Investigation

1. **SageMath dependency:** The Calabi-Yau intersection computation (Thrust I) was blocked by the absence of SageMath. The geometric bridge between adelic cross-ratios and compactification data remains unexplored.

2. **Quark sector precision:** Current uncertainties on $u$, $d$, $s$ quark masses ($\pm 25\%$, $\pm 22\%$, $\pm 8\%$ respectively) prevent rigorous testing of adelic constraints in the quark sector.

3. **Scope:** This investigation focused on the Freund-Witten adelic Veneziano amplitude. Other adelic constructions (adelic $L$-functions, automorphic forms, Shimura varieties) may yield different constraints.

## 6. Conclusion

We have conducted a systematic, three-phase investigation of adelic constraints on quantum field theory, employing a rigorous methodology of code-executed computation, explicit evidence classification, and honest falsification of negative results. The principal conclusions are:

1. **The adelic framework is the Riemann zeta function** in the language of adeles. $\Gamma_\infty(x) = \zeta(1-x)/\zeta(x)$ exactly. The adelic product formula $\equiv 1$ is the zeta functional equation — a mathematical identity, not a separate physical theory.

2. **The framework provides structural constraints** that are mathematically well-defined and physically meaningful: rational Veneziano cross-ratios, bounded RG flow, Landau pole cancellation by $p$-adic compensation.

3. **The framework does not provide specific numerical predictions** for fermion masses. The $\log(m_\mu/m_e) \approx 16/3$ coincidence, which appeared to connect the QED beta function to the muon-electron mass ratio, has been conclusively falsified: it cannot be derived from the adelic structure (10 independent attempts failed) and does not extend to the tau lepton ($\sim 20,000$ tested patterns, none survived Bonferroni correction).

4. **"Fundamental constants" extracted from mathematical frameworks are suspect.** Both $R = 8.44$ (Phase 1) and $\log(m_\mu/m_e) = 16/3$ (Phases 2–3) collapsed under scrutiny — the former as a normalization-dependent artifact, the latter as a numerological coincidence. Cross-ratios, not individual transcendental values, are the appropriate mathematical objects.

The adelic framework is best characterized as a **structural meta-theory of quantum field theory**. It constrains the *form* of physical laws — their functional equations, their RG boundedness, their invariant structures — without determining their specific coefficient values, which depend on contingent features such as compactification geometry, gauge group representations, and initial conditions.

This is a scientifically valuable outcome: it clarifies the boundary between what mathematical structures can and cannot determine about physical observables. The search for mathematical constraints on quantum field theory continues — but the constraints will be structural, not numerical.
